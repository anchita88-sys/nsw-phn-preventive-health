# References

## Primary data sources

1. **Australian Institute of Health and Welfare (AIHW).** *Primary health care: Medicare Benefits Schedule and other universal health-related services* — Data tables PHC 019 (2017–18 to 2024–25).  
   - File in project: `01_RawData/aihw-phc-019-data-tables-1718-2425.xlsx`  
   - Metric used: GP (general practice) Medicare services per population by PHN  

2. **AIHW.** *BreastScreen Australia monitoring* and supplementary data tables.  
   - Files: `AIHW-CAN-158-Access-to-BreastScreen-Australia-screening-services-2024-02-16.xlsx`, CAN-165 screening tables  
   - Metric used: Participation rate (%) among eligible women aged 50–74 by PHN  

3. **Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS).** *Census of Population and Housing, 2021* — General Community Profile (GCP).  
   - Files: `GCP_*.xlsx`, `GCP_AUS.xlsx`  
   - Metrics: overseas-born, recent migrants, non-English speaking households, family complexity  

4. **AIHW / ABS patient experience surveys.** Delayed GP access when needed (NSW aggregate, 34.4%) — included as equity context indicator in project narrative.

## Definitions

- **GP services per 100 people:** Count of Medicare Benefits Schedule (MBS) billed GP consultation services per 100 population in a reporting year. This is a *utilisation rate*, not a count of GP doctors.  
- **BreastScreen participation (%):** Proportion of eligible women (typically 50–74) who participated in BreastScreen Australia in the reporting period.  
- **PHN:** Primary Health Network — regional primary care commissioning boundary.
