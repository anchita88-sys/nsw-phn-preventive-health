GP Utilisation vs Preventive Screening Gap

Follow-on analysis. Medicare GP statistics and cancer screening participation. NSW PHNs.

South Western Sydney, Nepean Blue Mountains, and Western Sydney combine the highest GP use with the lowest average screening across BreastScreen, bowel (NBCSP), and cervical (NCSP) programs.

What this analysis adds

The main report showed high GP billing but low BreastScreen uptake in Western Sydney. This report checks whether that pattern also shows up in bowel and cervical screening.

The gap is broader than breast screening alone. Bowel screening is especially low in the highest-gap PHNs (35-37%).

Methods

GP-screening gap = GP Medicare services per 100 people ÷ mean of three screening participation rates (%).

A higher gap means more GP activity relative to organised screening uptake.

Policy implications

  • Target bowel and breast outreach together in Western Sydney, SW Sydney, and Nepean Blue Mountains.
  • Do not treat high GP contact as proof that prevention is working. Use it to prompt program-specific recall.
  • Add Nepean Blue Mountains to Western Sydney equity planning. It ranks second on the gap measure.
